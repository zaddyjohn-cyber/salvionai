FAQ

Questions, answered.

How accurate is a camera-based scan?
Salvion's transformer models are engineered to match or exceed dedicated medical hardware for core vitals, with every parameter returned alongside a confidence score. The platform is currently in clinical validation across Southeast Asia — results are intended for screening and wellness insight, not standalone diagnosis.
What hardware does it need?
Any standard smartphone or laptop camera — no wearables, sensors or accessories. Inference runs on-device in sub-50ms on modern iOS and Android hardware, or in your private cloud where you prefer.
Where does the video data go?
Nowhere by default. Processing is 100% local — raw video never leaves the device. Only the resulting health markers are returned, and the architecture is HIPAA- and GDPR-compliant by design, with regional data residency available on cloud deployments.
How long does integration take?
Most teams ship a working scan in days, not months. Drop in the Web, iOS or Android SDK, call a single scan() method, and receive FHIR R4 output ready for your EHR or data pipeline. White-label UI and custom pipelines are available for deeper builds.
Which markers can it measure?
50+ markers across six domains — vital signs, physiological, physical, blood proxies, body composition and mental wellness — plus the Salvion Health Score™, risk tiers and Early Risk Signals™. You can bill per parameter and enable only what your product needs.
Is it compliant for regulated markets?
Salvion supports HIPAA, GDPR, FHIR and OJK (Indonesia), with PDPI alignment and an ISO 13485 roadmap. Enterprise deployments add dedicated tenancy, SLA-backed routing and full audit logging.
